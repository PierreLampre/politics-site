WAYNE T. ZANNI

Running for:
Vineland School Board
Incumbent
Challenger

Website

individual agenda

He affirms that Vineland's students must have access to a high-quality, rigorous education that prepares them for success in career, college, and life. His goal is to place the school's academic culture first. He wants to represent the consensus of Vineland's families values, beliefs, and traditions.

policy positions

He wants to prioritize subjects like language literacy and math literacy over topics like gender.
He wants to ensure that kids are prepared to compete for jobs with an ever changing demographic in the nation.
He wants to prevent and eliminate liberal, progressive indoctrination, such as critical race theory, gender identity, and transgender inclusion.
He will to protect and encourage students.
He will defend parental rights.
He will respect and support teachers, and the school support staff.
He will wisely spend tax dollars.
He will follow family values built from Abrahamic principles.
Has quoted a law regarding that teachers should protect the welfare of the child as the parent desires. Says that school systems and teachers are misinterpreting this law to effectively replace the parents.
Has mentioned that VHS ranked 330th out of 350 schools in the state, so he wants to hold the superintendent accountable for the school's poor academic performance.
He says that school attendance is at about 85% and that this is not an acceptable standard. Imagine if you ran a business and your employee missed approximately 1 day of work per week, that's essentially what 85% attendance yields.
Says that social engineering programs were snuck in during the Obama administration. He would rather "let children be children."

experience

Born and raised in Vineland. Taught in the Millville school system. He is a parent and grandparent. He is a tax-paying, fiscally responsible senior citizen. He is a formally trained educator for topics such as ethics, history, law, methods, philosophy, and psychology. He has experience teaching all grade levels and adults in reading, civics, history, and geography. He is a summer literacy coordinator for the high school level with a 100% pass rate. He is an assistant varsity high school coach with boys and girls teams reaching county and conference championships. He has adjunct professor with 20+ years teaching World Geography.
