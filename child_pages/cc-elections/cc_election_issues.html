County Issues

An organized list of issues of contention this election cycle.

County Level Issues

Middle Mile Broadband
This has been the most popular issue over the past several months. As part of the Inflation Reduction Act, Senator Van Drew applied for Cumberland County to receive a federal grant to upgrade our broadband infrastructure. The full grant was approximately $35 million, which Cumberland County would need to spend about $5 million of its own money. The controversy over moving forward on this project was that the ‘middle mile’ grant only extends dark fiber around the general area of the county but does not guarantee that any homes will actually be hooked up in the future. That part would rely on internet companies such as Comcast and Verizon, or individual residents, to finish the connection. There was also no feasibility study finished. There was much passionate debate at county meetings on whether to move forward or not, and finally the county commission voted in favor of moving forward. Since the vote, Director Commissioner Albrecht informed me that the county is already in talks to receive ‘end mile’ money, which would help individual customers to connect their specific homes.
County Jail
There seems to be several problems revolving around the county jail. The most obvious of which is an ongoing class action lawsuit about the mistreatment of prisoners during the pandemic. This seems to have led to some discussion about renovating the jail while focusing on issues such as mental health, rehabilitation, etc.
Nabb Ave Extension
Nabb Ave is a road in Millville which is close to both its business and industrial districts. Extending it is proposed to have an effect in which trucks delivering goods via route 55 would have easier access, which might reduce transportation costs, which could help overall business in the county.
Sewage/Septic infrastructure
The county seems to have outdated sewage and septic systems, which leads to many problems regarding business development and home real estate (such as building new homes or selling homes). I’ve been quoted costs anywhere from $15,000 - $90,000 for sewage/septic upgrades of individual properties.
Tourist Destination Goals
Cumnberland County has been working with Cape May County to promote general tourism in the area. With Cape May already having an established tourism industry, many tourists and vacationers book hotels in Cumberland County during their trips. Both counties are working together to increase tourism in Cumberland County, as that will help Cumberland County apply for state tourism grants, provide tourism packages for both counties, and help develop business in both counties.
Homelessness
Homeslessness is on the rise throughout the country and Cumberland County is no exception. Areas of Landis Avenue in Vineland and the business districts in Bridgeton or Millville are examples of the problem growing.
Crime
Much like homelessness, crime seems to be a growing problem in the county. With discussion of gangs in Bridgeton regularly occurring at county meetings, what are our candidates planning to do to handle crime?
Dangerous Off-road vehicles
Off-road vehicles such as dirt-bikes and ATVs have been a problem in the county for years. When addressed at county meetings, commissioners claim that the police’s hands are tied because of insurance and state law related issues. Will we ever solve this problem?

State Level Issues

Immigration – Sanctuary State Status
With New Jersey being a sanctuary state and the recent influx in NYC, it has been rumored that a plane full of migrants will be flown to the Atlantic City airport. Should they day arise, are we prepared to handle our own influx of migrants?
Trans Rights
The topic of trans rights has been a hot topic for a while… where do the candidates stand on the following commonly debated sub-issues:
  1. School Sports
  2. Gendered bathrooms
  3. Trans people and gender specific prisons
  4. Parents rights vs school policy
Renewable Energy and Wind Turbines
A recurring topic in the news is how the recent wind turbines which have been installed off-shore could be endangering the lives of whales, many of which have washed ashore.
Election Integrity and Electoral College Slate of Electors Authenticity
One of Donald Trump’s indictments has to do with an alleged alternate slate of electors being used to call the 2020 election into question. Exactly how do our party’s slate of electors work and what can our candidates tell us to ensure us that no fraudlent electors are meddling with our elections? Furthermore, what can our candidates tell us in regards to ensuring us overall election integrity will be upheld?
Abortion Policy
With the recent overturning of Roe v Wade, what are our candidates exact stances on abortion policy? More specifically, what are their ideal policies vs their hard lines with respect that both sides of the aisle may ultimately need to come to a compromise?
Covid Policy
With rumors that mask mandates and boosters may be returning, how do our candidates feel about the implementation of covid policy?
Budget and Taxes
With our state reaching $200 billion in debt, how will our candidates ensure us that they will push for fiscal responsibility?
Healthcare
With universal healthcare being a recurring topic, how do our candidates propose to handle healthcare moving forward?
Education
With many areas throughout the country having education challenges, what plans do our candidates have to address this issue?
Infrastructure
With our county being way behind on issues like access to broadband and water/sewage standards, what are our candidates plans to improve overall infrastructure?
Gun Rights
With gun rights being a recurring topic, where do our candidates stand on the issue?
