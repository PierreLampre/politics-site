quick summary

County Commissioner

This year 2 seats are open on the County Commissioner board. The republicans are running two incumbents, Director Commissioner Sileo and Commissioner Romero. The democrats are running two challengers, former Commissioner Capizola and former Sheriff Austino. Both parties appear to be running 'slate', which means they are running as a team within their parties, potentially all the way up to the national level. All candidates have been reached out to in terms of clarifying their individual characteristics, agendas, and comparisons to other candidates. None have responded. So, if you vote party line, as of now, that's fair. If you're more of an independent who votes for individuals as opposed to parties, their is no significant information as of yet.

The two republican incumbents for County Commissioner inherently have experience, so it is the opinion of Inform the Vote that there ought to be some kind of discussion as to why the Democrat challengers would be significantly better or different than the incumbents, especially in terms of the specific local roles they will hold. It's difficult to assess how candidates will perform differently from each other with only similar bios among candidates and a 'party-line' approach when many party-line talking points might not directly apply at the local level or the role of County Commissioner.
