County Commissioner

Two seats are open on the Cumberland County Commissioners Board. The two republican incumbents, Commissioner Albrecht and Commissioner Lods, are running unopposed in the republican primary. While there is general campaign information on the Cumberland County Regular Republican site and some specifics come up from time to time at the bi-weekly republican breakfasts, there are a lot more specifics regarding known issues in the county that ought to be talked about directly. We have reached out to both candidates and Albrecht plans on corresponding, yet we have not received anything after months of waiting.

On the democrat side, three candidates are competing for two seats. John Capizola is a former commissioner who was appointed to his seat mid-term. Yolanda Balicki is a thoroughly credentialed immigration lawyer. Larae Smith is a known political figure in the community who has run for office multiple times in recent years. One would think they would have a vested interest in making their cases clear to the public, yet we have received no response from our efforts to make contact.

As the Democrat primary approaches, time is quickly slipping away for candidates to make their case to the public. Meanwhile, Republican candidates still have ample time before the general election to engage with voters. But regardless of the timeline, the kind of basic information we’ve been seeking—positions on issues, general agendas, and comparisons to their well known opponents—should already be available. After two years of repeated attempts to gather this information and foster cooperation, we’ve learned not to expect a lot. Simply put: we’re not holding our breath for a sudden outbreak of transparency.

What does a commissioner do?

A county commissioner is an elected official responsible for overseeing the administration of county government, like an extension of the state government that oversees the specific county. The role can vary slightly depending on the specific laws and regulations of the state, but generally, county commissioners have the following responsibilities:

Learn More Budgeting and Finance: County commissioners are responsible for creating and approving the county's budget. They allocate funds to various departments and services, such as law enforcement, public health, infrastructure, and education.

Policy Making: They set policies for the county, including zoning laws, land use planning, and local ordinances. Commissioners may also pass resolutions that impact county operations and the well-being of residents.

Infrastructure and Public Works: Commissioners oversee the maintenance and development of county infrastructure, such as roads, bridges, parks, and public buildings. They may also be involved in decisions regarding public transportation and utilities.

Public Safety and Health: They ensure that services like law enforcement, fire protection, emergency medical services, and public health departments are adequately funded and managed.

Taxation: County commissioners have the authority to levy taxes and determine the tax rate for the county. They ensure that sufficient revenue is generated to fund county services.

Community Services: They oversee various community services, including social services, libraries, and recreation programs.

Economic Development: Commissioners often work on initiatives to promote economic growth in the county, including attracting businesses, supporting local agriculture, and fostering job creation.

Representation: County commissioners serve as representatives of the residents in their district or the county at large, depending on how the commission is structured. They listen to constituents' concerns and advocate for their needs at the county level.

Environmental and Land Use Issues: Commissioners may be involved in decisions related to environmental protection, land conservation, and the regulation of natural resources.

Collaboration with Other Governments: County commissioners often work with state and local governments, as well as regional authorities, to coordinate services and address issues that cross jurisdictional boundaries.

Overall, county commissioners play a crucial role in shaping the policies and services that affect the daily lives of county residents.
