What does a Township Committee Member do?

Learn More A township committee member serves on the governing body of a township, which is a form of municipal government commonly found in New Jersey. The roles and responsibilities of township committee members include the following:

Legislative Responsibilities: Township committee members are responsible for enacting ordinances, resolutions, and regulations that govern the township. They make decisions on local laws, zoning, land use, and other matters that impact the community.

Budgeting and Financial Oversight: Committee members participate in the development and approval of the township’s annual budget. This involves allocating funds to various departments, services, and projects, as well as setting tax rates and managing financial resources.

Executive Functions: The township committee also has executive responsibilities, such as appointing key officials (like the township administrator, attorney, or engineer) and overseeing the implementation of policies and ordinances. In some townships, one committee member may serve as the mayor, who presides over meetings and represents the township in official matters.

Public Services and Infrastructure: Committee members oversee the provision of public services, including road maintenance, public safety, parks and recreation, and other essential services. They work to ensure that infrastructure projects are completed and that services are delivered efficiently.

Community Representation: As elected officials, township committee members represent the interests and concerns of the township’s residents. They engage with the community, attend public meetings, and respond to constituents' concerns to ensure that the local government is addressing the needs of the people.

Strategic Planning: Committee members are involved in long-term planning for the township, which may include land use planning, economic development initiatives, and efforts to improve the quality of life for residents.

Collaboration: In townships with a committee form of government, decisions are often made collaboratively, with members voting on important issues. The committee typically operates on a majority vote basis, where the collective input of the members shapes township policies and initiatives.

Quick Election Intro

Learn More

Fairfield Township voters will choose two Township Committee members in the upcoming election. The race features incumbents Benjamin A. Byrd Sr. and Marvin R. Pierce Jr. running for re-election, opposed by independent candidates Kenneth E. Brent and Darlington Henry.



Byrd and Pierce currently sit on the Township Committee, with Pierce serving as Mayor. Both have established roles in Fairfield’s local government and community.



Kenneth E. Brent and Darlington Henry appear on the ballot as independents. Publicly available information on their backgrounds and platforms is limited at this time; we sent emails to the incumbents on August 25th with no response as of yet, and we plan on attending the next township committee meeting on September 17th to make everyone aware of our outreach efforts during public comments. InformTheVoteNJ is actively researching and will update this section as more details emerge.
