Gubernatorial Race

The governor's race this year is highly anticipated and has lots of competitors. To be clear, we should not be any sort of authoritative source on this race. Rather, we are going to consolidate and present the info as best as we can, but ultimately it will be up to you to sift through all the information.

Phil Murphy is not seeking re-election, and the democrats have 6 people in their primary race. While a lot could be said about the strengths and weaknesses of all the candidates, one easy way to get an idea of who they are is to watch their primary debate:

Click to explore: Candidate Spectrum – From Status Quo to System Reformer

This guide organizes candidates from the most establishment-oriented to the most radically reform-minded — helping voters choose based on leadership style and appetite for change.


Steve Sweeney – Status Quo Steward
  • ✅ Deepest state-level experience (Former Senate President)
  • ⚙️ Governance Style: Institutional, legislative, incremental
  • 🗣️ Tone: Moderate and procedural

  • 🎯 Best fit for voters who believe the system just needs better management
  • More about Steve Sweeney →
Josh Gottheimer – Suburban Technocrat
  • 🏛️ Federal experience (Congressman, “Problem Solver Caucus”)
  • 💼 Governance Style: Incrementalist with fiscal focus
  • 🗣️ Tone: Moderate, centrist talking points

  • 🎯 Appeals to voters who want affordability without major disruption
  • More about Josh Gottheimer →
Mikie Sherrill – Disciplined Operator
  • 🇺🇸 Former Navy pilot and U.S. Attorney; current Congresswoman
  • ⚖️ Governance Style: Federalist, procedural, institution-trusting
  • 🗣️ Tone: Polished, cautious

  • 🎯 Suits voters seeking stable leadership with national-level credibility
  • More about Mikie Sherrill →
Sean Spiller – Union-Focused Populist
  • 🏫 NJEA President and Montclair Mayor
  • 🛠️ Governance Style: Public-sector progressive, union-first policymaker
  • 🗣️ Tone: Passionate, activist-leaning

  • 🎯 Appeals to voters who want stronger labor influence without tearing down institutions
  • More about Sean Spiller →
Steve Fulop – Policy-Focused Reformer
  • 🏙️ Jersey City Mayor, former Marine and Goldman Sachs employee
  • 🔨 Governance Style: Bold reformer with detailed platform
  • 🗣️ Tone: Confrontational, issue-driven

  • 🎯 Ideal for voters who want major structural reform grounded in administrative experience
  • More about Steve Fulop →
Ras Baraka – Radical Equity Advocate
  • 🏙️ Newark Mayor with a transformative local record
  • 🧱 Governance Style: Structural reform rooted in racial equity and public ownership
  • 🗣️ Tone: High-activist, movement-aligned

  • 🎯 Resonates with voters who believe New Jersey needs fundamental change and redistribution
  • More about Ras Baraka →
