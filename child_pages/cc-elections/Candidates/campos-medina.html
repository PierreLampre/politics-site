Patricia Campos-Medina

Patricia Campos-Medina is a first-generation American who wants to help ensure the American dream is available to future generations. She has been a union organizer, a community organizer, and an advocate for many causes for over 30 years. She has received appointments from former presidents Clinton, Bush, and Obama. experience

She was appointed to Labor Advisory Committee for US Trade relations in 2000 by President Clinton and reappointed in 2004 by President Bush. In 2008 she was appointed to the Transition Team for the Office of Trade Relations by President Obama. In 2004 she led a successful collective bargaining strike for casino workers in Atlantic City, and was then made the New Jersey State Director for UNITE HERE, the union that led the strike. Served on boards and commissions such as the NJ Casino Redevelopment Authority, the NJ Economic Growth Council, the NY-NJ Port Security Task Force, and the NJ Restart and Recovery Advisory Council (related to COVID). She was the executive director of the Worker Institute at ILR-Cornell University where she focused on collective bargaining rights. She led programs to prevent harassment against women and LGBTQIA+ in workplaces, to address the gender pay gap, to increase work safety for nurses, and to increase labor rights for domestic workers. Led efforts to increase the amount of women in appointed offices.
