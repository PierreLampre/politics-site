Lawrence Hamm

Lawrence Hamm has been an advocate for African-Americans, human rights, and civil rights for 52 years. At age 20 he was the youngest person ever to be appointed to the Newark city council. He has a master's in business administration and was president of the 24th district assembly. He also has a lot of experience in political leadership and faith-based organizations. experience

Hamm has been an advocate for African-Americans, human rights, and civil rights for 52 years. Starting at 17, he was an advocate for education. He was a Newark Board of Education member. At 20 he qualified to be a councilman for Newark. He was an organizer for the anti-apartheid movement while attending Princeton, which resulted in the university divesting in the South African economy. He has a master’s in business administration. He was president of the 24th District Assembly. He was the founder and director of the People’s Energy Cooperative. He was director of the Community Organization Program of the United Church of Christ, Commission for Racial Justice. He was co-chair of Jesse Jackson’s presidential campaign in NJ. He was president of the NJ Rainbow Coalition. He was chairman of the NJ Million Man March Coalition. He was president of the NJ Chapter of the Conference of Minority Transportation Officials. He was chairman of the People’s Organization for Progress (social justice). He received Key to the City of Newark.
