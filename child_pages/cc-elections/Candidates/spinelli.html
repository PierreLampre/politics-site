quick summary

Paul F. Spinelli

Current Council President Spinelli has an extensive resume, and most notably for his re-election bid he served on the school board for 3 years including its finance committee, the council for 12 years including 4 years a president, and numerous community based positions from being an educator/principal, to a basketball coach, volunteer and liason to his parish, and much more. Given his current role as council president, he has a detailed agenda including bringing better paying jobs and businesses to the city in a controlled manner, update infrastructure such as water/sewer/storm water drains, continue to negotiate fair and equitable contracts for employees, and more. He highlights that he has been in leadership positions his whole life and that he has been successful in his current role, so he proposes that a continuation of his tenure will continue to bring success. He also presents himself as a good balance of being detailed oriented and a down-to-earth approach that helps residents solve problems that they couldn't otherwise solve. individual agenda

  • Continue to negotiate with businesses to bring them into the city in a controlled manner and bring better jobs to the area
  • Continue to update outdated ordinances and make them relevant to the present city environment
  • Make it a point to continue with upgrading the infrastructure of our city and bring it up to date; water, sewer, storm water drains etc
  • Continue to fix and upgrade our city owned buildings so our citizens have a better experience when visiting and using them Examples: senior center, Library, Fire, Police and EMS buildings.
  • Continue to negotiate fair and equitable contracts for both the taxpayer and our employees in the future.
  • Continue to bring events to Vineland that will provide a better life experience and quality of living for the entire population of the city.

    experience

    Bio presented by Spinelli:

    Paul is a retired educator for over 40 years of service. He has served as a Vineland City Council Member for over 12 years, four as the President. He has also served on the Vineland Board of Education for 3 years and chaired the finance committee.

    Paul is married to Josephine Orlando-Spinelli and has a son Paul F Spinelli and his wife Heather, who have two children Angelo and Stella his grandchildren.

    He graduated from Sacred Heart High School; His undergraduate work was at Mt. St. Mary’s University with a Bachelor of Science Degree in History Education and Political Science Graduating Cum Laude. He received his Masters Degree in Supervision and Administration graduating Summa Cum Laude from the University of Phoenix.

    Various positions held by Paul during his career were: Teacher, Principal, Vice Principal, member of the Vineland PAL board for 8 years, member of the Vineland Downtown Improvement District for over 12 years, Teacher of the year in 2002 in Atlantic City Public Schools, Senior Vice President of the Atlantic City Education Association for 8 years, Mentor teacher for over 12 years while helping young educators assimilate to the teaching profession while in Atlantic City Public Schools.

    Positions held in Youth Sports: Varsity baseball coach in Atlantic City High School, Coach in the Vineland Soccer Association for 4 years, coach in the Vineland Little League, Coach in the Vineland Mini Wrestling program, Basketball Interpreter 8 years, Secretary and Assigner for 28 years of the Cumberland-Cape May Board of Basketball Officials 196, College basketball official for over 10 years, Life time member of International Association of Basketball Officials, President , Vice President, President Elect, and Executive Board Member for over 12 years of the International Association of Basketball Officials, basketball official of the year on 2012 in the State of New Jersey and in the Basketball Hall of Fame in Springfield Mass., for being President of the Association. Member of the NJSIAA and State evaluator, member of the College Basketball officials Organization

    Community organizations: Vineland Jaycees member and Vice President, winning the Key Man Award from the organization, volunteer at Christ the Good Shepherd Parish in charge of capital projects, Vineland VBA in the inception year, Recreation Commission for 8 years, liaison to Christ the Good Shepherd Parish, member North Italy Club

    With the above resume Paul has influenced thousands of young people in making the best choices for themselves in life, whether it was education, sports or in the political realm. As councilman Paul has attempted to help anyone who was in need and had a viable issue that could be dealt with.
