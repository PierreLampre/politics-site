Erik Simonsen

Erik Simonsen is a New Jersey Assemblyman representing the 1st Legislative District since 2020. A lifelong educator and former mayor of Lower Township, he brings experience in local governance and school administration. Simonsen currently serves on multiple Assembly committees, including Education and State and Local Government. With a background in special education and community leadership, he emphasizes conservative fiscal policy, public safety, and support for small businesses and families in South Jersey.

Experience

Erik Simonsen is a current New Jersey Assemblyman representing District 1, first sworn in on January 14, 2020. He previously served as Mayor of Lower Township and held positions on the township council. Professionally, he works in education as the Athletic Director for the Lower Township Regional School District and has previously served as an assistant principal and special education teacher. Simonsen holds degrees in education administration, special education, and general education. He is also an ordained deacon and former vice president of the Cape May County NAACP.
