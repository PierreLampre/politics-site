quick summary

Tony Romero

Commissioner Romero is up for re-election this year as the only person of color running at the county level. Being that he is an incumbent, he inherently has more experience than his democrat challengers. Being of Cuban descent, he is particularly focused on standing against communism by upholding the constitution. He is also particularly passionate about cutting red tape for businesses. Like most candidates at the local level, Romero seems to be running slate (as part of the overall republican team) and there is little forward facing information about how he is directly different from his democrat challengers. He has been reached out to via his government email to clarify his individual stances and how he differentiates himself from his opponents, with no response. party messaging

After reaching out to multiple members of the CCRRO, it is not totally clear how the Republican party platform applies to Cumberland County. However, could infer some of the following:
  • Opposition to policy 5756 in the school system (the notorious 'Trans rights vs Parents rights' issue) suggests pushing for socially conservative values. Local Lens Article
  • Opposition to projects like a 50+ million dollar French Museum in Jersey City suggests prioritization on fiscal conservatism. New York Times Article

policy positions

After reaching out to Commissioner Romero and not receiving a response, it is unclear what specific policy positions relevant to Cumberland County he has moving forward. However, we can infer some of the following having paid attention a fair amount over the past year:
  • Romero voted no to proceed with the Middle Mile Broadband Project. In his explanation he gave reasons regarding the cost and management of the project, which he said was informed by his years of experience in the cable broadband industry. This suggests he will error on the side of fiscal conservatism and sound business management. Cumberland County Work Session Video
  • While Romero might not have addressed any specific policy positions regarding social issues in Cumberland County, he has made some comments at breakfast meetings which suggest he would move in the direction of socially conservative values.
