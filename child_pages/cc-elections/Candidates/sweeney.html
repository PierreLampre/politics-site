Steve Sweeney

Steve Sweeney is a veteran public servant with over two decades of experience in state and county government, including more than a decade as President of the New Jersey Senate. A former ironworker turned legislator, Sweeney brings a deeply personal and practical approach to politics—shaped by his working-class roots and advocacy for people with disabilities following the birth of his daughter with Down Syndrome.

As a candidate for governor, Sweeney is campaigning on a wide-ranging platform focused on infrastructure upgrades, lower housing costs, no new taxes, stronger public safety, disability rights, and affordable energy. His proposals include expanding apprenticeships, restoring COLA for retirees, cutting red tape for housing development, and reversing energy fund allocations to benefit homeowners. He also supports community policing, job training for the formerly incarcerated, and increased funding for mental health and senior services.

Notably, Sweeney has a strong legislative record backing workers, families, and vulnerable populations—authoring New Jersey’s Paid Family Leave law, sponsoring marriage equality, and defending women’s reproductive rights. He’s the only candidate with a long record of statewide impact, clear policy positions, and a history of getting things done.

Relevant Bio and Experience – Steve Sweeney


Background and Profession:

  • Born in Camden, raised in Pennsauken.

  • Began his career as an ironworker, grounding him in labor and working-class values.


Personal Motivation:

  • Inspired to serve after his daughter was born with Down Syndrome, leading to lifelong advocacy for people with disabilities and vulnerable communities.


County-Level Experience:

  • Served on the Gloucester County Board of Chosen Freeholders from 1997 to 2010.

  • Held the position of Freeholder Director (1998–2010), implementing taxpayer savings programs totaling $30 million.


State Legislative Experience:

  • New Jersey State Senator (2002–2021), representing District 3 (Gloucester, parts of Cumberland, and all of Salem counties).

  • Elected Senate President in 2009 and led the Democratic caucus for over a decade.


Legislative Achievements:

  • Authored New Jersey’s Paid Family Leave program.

  • Spearheaded legislation for:
    • Minimum wage increases
    • Property tax relief
    • Equal pay for women
    • Marriage equality
    • Special education reform
    • Sexual Assault Bill of Rights
    • Stronger child protection laws involving sex offenders
