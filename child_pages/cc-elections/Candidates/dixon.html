Dan Dixon

Dan Dixon is a lifelong Millville resident, business owner, and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu coach running for Millville City Commission in the 2025 election. With decades of experience in sales, marketing, and team leadership, as well as advanced degrees in exercise science and human performance, Dixon brings a research-driven, business-minded approach to city government.

His top priority is tackling crime as the foundation for Millville’s revitalization, proposing a detailed 10-point plan that includes hiring more police officers, reestablishing the Crime Task Force, targeting nuisance properties, and implementing proactive community safety measures. He also emphasizes creative strategies for increasing city revenue—such as aggressive code enforcement, Special Improvement Districts, and a dedicated commercial property platform—to attract businesses and grow the local economy.

Positioning himself as a fresh voice unbound by political games, Dixon pledges transparency, collaboration, and a commitment to making Millville a safer, stronger, and more prosperous city.

Overall Agenda and Positions

  • Public Safety and Crime Reduction

    Dan Dixon’s top priority is addressing crime as the foundation for Millville’s revitalization. He argues that economic growth is impossible without first making Millville safer for families and businesses.

    His 10-point plan for public safety includes:

    • Hiring 10–15 additional police officers to fully staff the department.
    • Reestablishing the Crime Task Force to target violent crime, drug activity, and gangs.
    • Deploying mobile or semi-permanent precincts in high-crime areas.
    • Passing a content-neutral traffic safety ordinance to address standing in high-traffic areas and enforcing private property panhandling restrictions.
    • Holding slumlords accountable for unsafe and nuisance properties through stricter penalties and enforcement.
    • Seizing and redeveloping long-abandoned or non-compliant properties.
    • Working with regional partners to relocate homeless individuals to facilities with appropriate resources.
    • Implementing drug, anti-gang, and crime prevention education programs in schools.
    • Placing limits on new affordable housing development to prevent over-concentration and strain on city resources.
    • Collaborating with county, state, and federal agencies to dismantle drug networks in Millville.
    Dixon also emphasizes evidence-based strategies, such as focused deterrence programs, community engagement, environmental design improvements, and public health approaches to reduce crime, citing successful examples from other U.S. cities.


  • Economic Development and Revenue Growth

    Dixon believes revitalizing Millville requires both improving safety and creatively increasing city revenues without overburdening taxpayers.

    His proposals include:

    • Developing a city-managed commercial property platform to attract businesses and showcase Millville’s advantages.
    • Establishing Special Improvement Districts (SIDs) to fairly assess properties that disproportionately use city resources, reinvesting funds into security, beautification, and infrastructure.
    • Implementing aggressive code enforcement to hold absentee landlords accountable, generate fine revenue, and improve property values over time.
    • Partnering with private and public entities to foster economic growth through strategic redevelopment and targeted incentives.
    He draws inspiration from successful economic development initiatives in cities like Jersey City, Vineland, and Newark.


  • Leadership Philosophy

    Dixon positions himself as a candidate with a fresh, business-oriented perspective rather than a career politician. He emphasizes openness, accountability, and collaborative problem-solving as his guiding principles for city governance.

    He sees Millville’s challenges as requiring bold, creative action and invites residents to share ideas to shape solutions together.



Background and Campaign Message

My name is Dan Dixon, and I am a lifelong Millville resident seeking a four-year seat on the Millville City Commission in the November 2025 election. I am running because I believe Millville deserves new energy, fresh ideas, and leaders unafraid to tackle difficult problems head-on.

My mission is simple: to make Millville safer, stronger, and more appealing for residents, businesses, and visitors. I believe transparency and accountability are critical to rebuilding trust in local government, and I am committed to openly sharing my plans, progress, and challenges with the public.

I bring decades of experience in business leadership, team management, and community engagement, along with advanced academic training in evidence-based problem-solving. My passion for helping individuals and organizations achieve their potential drives my vision for Millville’s future: a thriving, secure, and prosperous city we can all be proud to call home.
