quick summary

Andy Kim

Andy Kim is a current Congressman who was a Rhoads Scholar, a national security expert at the Pentagon, and a civilan advisor to the military in Afghanistan. He has a mix of left and right leaning agenda items such as supporting universal healthcare and supporting pro-choice policies, while also supporting small businesses and reducing red tape. He has been a Congressman for about 5 years, so he clearly has the most directly relevant experience in comparison to the other candidates. He has a lot of information below regarding his overall agenda, but be sure to go over the Democrat Party Platform to get a broader view of how he might handle the position. individual agenda

    Healthcare

  • Wants universal healthcare so every American has access to care they need at a cost they can afford.

  • Wants to lower prescription drug prices.

  • Wants to protect people with pre-existing conditions

  • Wants to allow Medicare to negotiate lower drug prices

  • Wants to cap out of pocket drug costs for seniors at $2,000.

  • Wants the price of insulin capped at $35/month

  • Wants to expand medicare to include dental, vision, and hearing.

  • Wants to ensure and protect access to reproductive healthcare.

  • Wants permanent funding for children's health insurance plans.

  • Wants rural community health centers.

  • Wants to make it easier for seniors to receive home health care

Prior Achievements/Legislative Votes

    Small Businesses and Creating Jobs

  • The Microloan Improvement Act, designed to reduce red tape and provide more options for accessing loans

  • Helped over 10,000 small businesses in New Jersey access financial assistance during the pandemic

  • The CARES Act, which provided checks to Americans, the PPP (paycheck protection program), and emergency grants for small businesses. It also has a provision that helps eligible people pay health insurance preminums.

  • The American Rescue Plan Act, designed to help businesses and essential workers stay afloat during COVID

  • The BUILD act, designed to create more federal contracting opportunities for veteran-owned small businesses.

  • The Put Our Neighbors To Work Act, designed to have the Department of Defense hire more local workers for military construction projects and give small businesses more opportunities to compete

    • Protecting the Environment through the Inflation Reduction Act

  • Investments in American energy production, and does more to combat climate change than any bill in history.

  • Investments in agriculture, coastal restoration, clean energy, advanced manufacturing, and transportation that will benefit hardworking New Jersey families.

  • Cutting greenhouse gas emissions by 40% by 2030, making the United States well-positioned to meet our climate goals while also investing in our economy.

  • Saves families an average of $1,000 per year in energy costs and creates good paying jobs.
