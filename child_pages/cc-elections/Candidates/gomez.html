quick summary

Cruz Gomez

Candidate Gomez is a lifelong Vineland resident who has worked on the Vineland Board of Ed as a supervisor for nearly 30 years, has been the owner of Kingdom Finance for about 10 years, and has been operating a tax firm for nearly 10 years. He is the president of two non-profits, Kingdom Empowering Entrepreneur Network and Kingdom Covenant Ministry. He is the author of two books, serves on the Hispanic Advisory Board of RCSJ, and is an ordained minister. He has joined Mayor Fanucci's 'Proven-Leadership Proven Results' ticket in this election and hopes to continue the current administrations general agenda to keep the streets safe and create new developments. With his experience in finance he hopes to bring his expertise to the city council, which would help bring financial literacy to the community and help keep the cost of living the lowest in NJ. He also differentiates himself from the other candidates by being a lifelong resident who went from a life of poverty to an established entrepreneur, so he will be able to relate to the cries of the less fortunate and properly represent them in local government. individual agenda

Quoted directly from communication with candidate Gomez:

The current administration has done an amazing job of renewing a sense of pride. I look to help continue the pride in the city and keep the streets safe. With the City creating Growth and New developments I hope to bring information and financial literacy to the small companies that are occupying store fronts in the City Vineland. I would like to help the current administration make informed and fiscal responsible decisions to help the city maintain the lowest cost of living in NJ.


Comparison to Other Candidates

Quoted directly from communication with candidate Gomez:

The difference between myself and other City Council candidates is the fact that I have lived in the city my whole life through many circumstances. From living in poverty to now being an established entrepreneur. I know the cries of those that are less fortunate and I know the process that is essential in reaching purpose. I know how important information is and I will be able to carry their cries to the Council table and bring back necessary information. My spiritual convictions will also assure that we make conscious decisions.


experience

Quoted directly from communication with candidate Gomez:

Cruz Gomez Jr. is a lifelong Vineland resident. He is part of a family that came to Vineland from Puerto Rico and has become a fixture in the city. He has been married to his wife Linda for 25 years and they have two daughters Mikayla and Miranda.

Cruz graduated from Vineland High School and attended Cumberland County College and Dundalk Community College in Maryland. Cruz has worked for the Vineland Board of Education as a Supervisor for nearly 30 years. In 2014, Cruz opened his first business Kingdom Finance, where he has developed a passion to provide financial literacy to youth and young adults. In 2015, he opened a tax firm, A Quality Tax Service, where he supplies clients with all of their tax needs.

Cruz is president of two Non-Profits, Kingdom Empowering Entrepreneur Network and Kingdom Covenant Ministry. Along with his wife, they have devoted their life to their faith and to families in need. He is also the author of two books "The Mirage" and "Dreamer Awaken." Cruz currently serves on the Hispanic Advisory Board of RCSJ Cumberland Campus and is an Ordained Minister.
