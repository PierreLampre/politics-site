Background and Experience

Heather Simmons is a longtime public servant from Gloucester County with a background in both education and government. She currently serves in the New Jersey General Assembly, having taken office in 2024, following four terms as a Gloucester County Commissioner — including service as Deputy Director. She was the longest-serving woman in that role.

Professionally, Simmons has held leadership roles at Rowan College of South Jersey and Rowan University, focusing on workforce innovation, strategic planning, and institutional partnerships. She holds a bachelor’s degree in international relations and politics and a master’s degree in public relations.

Her public service resume includes involvement in economic development, workforce training, public infrastructure, and education. She has also served on regional planning and civic boards across South Jersey. In her short time in the Assembly, she has already sponsored a wide range of bills touching on healthcare, property tax relief, environmental regulation, and labor protections — indicating a broad policy interest.

That said, we still encourage the candidate to clearly communicate her agenda for this election cycle so that voters don’t need to rely on speculation or research to understand where she stands.
