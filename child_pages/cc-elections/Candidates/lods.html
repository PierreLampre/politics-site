Victoria Lods

Victoria Groetsch-Lods is a first-term Cumberland County Commissioner with a wide-ranging background in communications, education, agriculture, and government. She’s worked as a high school educator, former chief of staff in the State Senate, and the first executive director of Cumberland County Habitat for Humanity. She also comes from a multi-generational farming family and has been active in local boards and public service throughout her career.

Since taking office, she has focused on issues like education, agriculture, mental health, and addiction services.

Lods is visible in the community and regularly attends local Republican events where she is approachable and willing to engage. However, her campaign lacks a centralized platform or clear public documentation of her current stances, making it difficult for voters to get a comprehensive view of her priorities without attending in-person events.

Given her communications background, there’s an opportunity for her to help set a new standard for public-facing engagement—but that shift has yet to fully take shape.

Rating Explanation

Victoria Lods

Victoria Lods is currently serving her first term on the Cumberland County Board of Commissioners. With a professional background that spans communications, education, state government, and agriculture, she brings a diverse and practical skill set to the board. Her previous roles include serving as chief of staff to a state senator, executive director of Cumberland County Habitat for Humanity, and a teacher at both Bridgeton and Millville High Schools. She’s also a fifth-generation resident of her family’s South Jersey farm, deeply rooted in the community she serves.

Lods has maintained a visible presence during her time in office, regularly attending Republican bi-weekly breakfast events and staying engaged with local constituents. When approached, she is friendly, articulate, and more than willing to converse—traits that make her approachable in person.

However, like most local candidates in this cycle, her public campaign infrastructure hasn’t caught up with what voters now expect in 2025. There’s currently no campaign website specifically for her, no comprehensive platform summary, and no participation in centralized efforts to provide side-by-side comparisons or deeper policy discussions.

And that’s the real disconnect—not just with Lods, but across the board: while face-to-face conversations and live events are valuable, they no longer satisfy the modern expectation of accessible, on-demand information. In a digital era where voters ought to be able track infrastructure projects like the Middle Mile Broadband initiative somewhat regularly, they’re also looking for candidates who proactively address those same issues with clear, documented perspectives... without necessarily reviewing every county, city, school board, and state meeting.

This becomes even more noticeable in Lods’ case, given her academic background in Communications and Journalism—fields built around the importance of message clarity and audience reach. One might expect someone with that training to do more to help bridge the gap between traditional campaigning and 21st-century civic transparency. Yet, we still lack a centralized reference point for her current priorities, stances, or comparisons to her opponents.

To be fair, she is far more present than candidates who remain completely out of view—but without more centrally accessible, digital campaign messaging, even an engaged candidate can feel distant to the average voter.

That said, the opportunity to improve remains fully open. A completed questionnaire, a podcast interview, and a detailed list of policy positions on key county issues would go a long way toward increasing her transparency score.

For these reasons, Victoria Groetsch-Lods currently receives an almost above average rating for transparency and accessibility.

Agenda

  • Education Advocacy: Continues to champion the role of schools and educators, both as a teacher and policymaker, supporting local students and promoting state-level funding for county districts.


  • Agricultural Representation: As a lifelong farmer, Lods brings direct knowledge of agricultural challenges and pushes for supportive local policies and economic opportunity in the farming sector.


  • Mental Health & Addiction Services: Vocal about expanding access to these services, though specific initiatives are not currently well-publicized online.

Issues

  • Mental Health & Addiction Services: Lods has been vocal about the importance of addressing mental health and addiction issues, particularly among students. She noted that many programs already exist to combat these crises and supports expanding awareness and access to them. Her recent attendance at the Maryville Recovery for Life Gala highlights her commitment to supporting recovery programs and recognizing leaders like Senator Michael Testa, who are working to improve access to treatment.


  • Community Engagement: Lods encourages more civic participation, especially among youth. She believes that community members should be involved in political discussions and campaigns and that public servants should remain visible and connected to the residents they serve.
